About Us

Barry Wilkinson

President/Co-Founder

Barry L. Wilkinson has spent the last 45 years serving the military services. After retiring from 24 years of active duty in 1996 as an Air Force First Sergeant he eventually returned to government service to work at the Offutt AFB Airman & Family Readiness Center (A&FRC) (formerly the Family Support Center). Barry spent the last 20 years overseeing the Personal Financial Readiness Program, Casualty Support, Department of Defense Transition Assistance Program (TAP), and the Executive and Chiefs TAP until his retirement in November 2017. During his tenure at the A&FRC he created the Offutt AFB Career Fair in partnership with over 250 local, national, and international companies. He is the co-founder of the Excel with a Mentor (EWAM) Program which is now a permanent part of the newly founded non-profit “Boots & Suits United, Inc. Inc.”
Barry holds a Bachelor’s Degree in Human Resources and Associates in Personnel Management along with national certification as an Accredited Financial Counselor (AFC®) through the Association of Financial, Counseling, and Planning Education (AFCPE).

Rafael Maldonado III

President/Co-Founder

Rafael Maldonado III is a strategic senior leader and change agent with comprehensive strategic planning, business development and consulting experience in multiple industries.
Rafael is a retired Air Force veteran who served as a Security Specialist, Computer Operator, Chief Facilitator Non-Commissioned Officer Academy, and First Sergeant over the course of twenty-two years of service. Upon retiring from military service Rafael has worked with fortune 500 and mid-level companies providing continuous consultation and training to associates, members of management staff, and executives relative to employee relations, compensation, benefits, staffing, and employment matters. He served as the primary contact to members of senior management to create results that were legally sound and of minimal risk. Rafael has also served as the liaison for the implementation of human resource programs, systems, and procedures to ensure that customer initiatives of the business units were met. Rafael is active in the community and is a member of multiple non-profit boards.
Rafael earned Master’s Degree from the University of Oklahoma in Human Relations. He holds two Bachelor’s degrees in Human Resource Management and Business Administration. He also holds two degrees from the Community College of the Air Force in Computer Science and Instructor Methodology. Rafael is a Certified Compensation Professional (CCP) and a Senior Professional Human Resources (SPHR)

Ken Waters

Director At Large

Kenneth “Ken” Waters is a senior leader with comprehensive experience in talent acquisitions, business development and consultation in multiple industries with a privately -owned staffing and search firm.
Kenneth is a retired Air Force Veteran who served as a Services Specialist, Superintendent of Mortuary Affairs, Honor Guard, Readiness and First Sergeant over the course of twenty-two years of service. Upon retiring from military service Ken has worked with small through large businesses offering comprehensive services to help through any phase of the employment cycle from finding and selecting top talent to managing and developing employees, to assisting employees through a career transition. He serves as a subject matter expert in the Light Industrial and Logistics Industry to senior management, assisting with executive recruiting and permanent or temporary staffing, in the Greater Omaha Area. Ken serves as an appointed Advisory Council member for Nebraska Future Business Leaders of America (FBLA).
Ken earned his Bachelor’s Degree from Ashford University in Organizational Management. He holds a degree from the Community College of the Air Force in Restaurant, Hotel and Fitness Management.

Molly

Secretary

Molly is proud Veteran of the United States Air Force. Today, she is an employee benefits consultant with Gallagher and has been an insurance professional for over 10 years. Her path to her current career was not met without challenge. She lacked insight as she transitioned into the civilian workforce. She parlayed her challenges into passion – a passion for ensuring others are equipped to tackle new opportunities the civilian sector provides. Through her work with the Excel with a Mentor Program, she challenges our Veteran’s to be the best version of themselves as they transition. Through preparation, perspective and a strong network it is her hope that everyone who goes through the program leaves feeling empowered to show the world all they have to offer.
